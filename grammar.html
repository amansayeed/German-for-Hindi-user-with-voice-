ЁЯТб Learning Tip: Understanding Verb Patterns

Regular Verbs Pattern: For most German verbs, remove the -en ending from the infinitive and add these endings:

  • du тЖТ -st (e.g., lernen тЖТ lernst)
  • er/sie/es тЖТ -t (e.g., lernen тЖТ lernt)
  • wir тЖТ -en (e.g., lernen тЖТ lernen)
  • ihr тЖТ -t (e.g., lernen тЖТ lernt)
  • Sie тЖТ -en (e.g., lernen тЖТ lernen)

ЁЯУЭ How to Use:

  1. Take any regular verb infinitive (e.g., spielen = to play, machen = to make)
  2. Remove the -en ending тЖТ spiel-, mach-
  3. Add the appropriate ending based on the pronoun
  4. Result: ich spiele, du spielst, er spielt, wir spielen, etc.

тЪая╕П Important: "sein" and "haben" are irregular verbs - they don't follow this pattern! Always memorize their conjugations separately.

ЁЯОп Memory Trick: The pattern -e, -st, -t, -en, -t, -en works for 90% of German verbs. Once you master this, you can conjugate hundreds of verbs!
